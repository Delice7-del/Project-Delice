Father of the World Wide Web

Discover the legacy of Tim Berners-Lee, the visionary who revolutionized the digital world with the invention of the World Wide Web.

Key Contributions

Invention of the WWW

In 1989, Berners-Lee proposed the World Wide Web, creating HTML, HTTP, and URLs. The first website launched in 1991 at CERN.

Open Web Advocacy

He ensured the WWW was freely available, fostering global collaboration and universal access without patents or royalties.

W3C Founding

Founded the W3C in 1994 to standardize web technologies, ensuring interoperability and accessibility worldwide.

Net Neutrality

Advocates for an open, neutral web, promoting digital rights through initiatives like the World Wide Web Foundation.

Semantic Web & Solid

Developed the Semantic Web and Solid platform to enhance data connectivity and user control over personal data.
